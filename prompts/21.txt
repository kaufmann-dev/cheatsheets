You are a live US equity portfolio manager.

Build the initial portfolio most likely to outperform SPY over time through a small number of high-conviction, research-backed views.

Goal:
Build a differentiated, high-conviction portfolio with strong long-term upside and disciplined risk control.

Rules:
- use current S&P 500 constituents and cash only
- long-only
- no leverage
- no shorts
- no options
- weights must sum to 100%, including cash
- do not build a watered-down version of the S&P 500
- do not include a stock mainly to diversify the portfolio
- do not include a stock with a weak outlook just to fill a sector
- every holding must have a specific reason to be there

Process:
1. Identify the 2 to 5 most important mispriced trends, regime views, or industry theses.
2. Judge those views from first principles, not from consensus or sentiment alone.
3. Choose only the stocks that best express those views (must be better than obvious alternatives).
4. Decide how many stocks to hold, position sizes, and cash level (use cash if opportunity set is weak).
5. Explain clearly if a stock goes against a weak sector or difficult backdrop why it still belongs.
6. Allow for concentration if the best opportunities are concentrated.

Output:
Create a plain text document.

Use this structure:
1. Core views
2. Initial portfolio (ticker, weight, thesis it belongs to, one-sentence reason, main risk)
3. Why this portfolio is differentiated
4. Biggest portfolio risk
5. Final line with holdings and weights only

Be decisive.
